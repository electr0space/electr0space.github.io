joy division

Joy Division were an English rock band formed in Salford in 1976. The group consisted of vocalist Ian Curtis, guitarist/keyboardist Bernard Sumner, bassist Peter Hook and drummer Stephen Morris.

While Joy Division's first recordings were heavily influenced by early punk, they soon developed a sparse, melancholy style that made them a pioneering act of the post-punk movement.

short history

Jun 4, 1976

Childhood Peter Hook and Bernard Sumner attended separately a Sex Pistols show. Inspired after the show, they decide to form a band. They advertise for a singer and hire Ian Curtis.

May 29, 1977

Bernard Sumner, Peter Hook, Ian Curtis and temporary drummer Tony Tabac play their first gig under their original name of Warsaw.

Jan 25, 1978

Group play first gig as Joy Division

Jun 1, 1978

Joy Division's self produced EP "Ideal For Living" is released.

Dec 1, 1978

On the way home from playing a gig, Ian Curtis suffers his first seizure and is diagnosed with epilepsy. This causes him depression and shame and fear that he is letting down the other members of the group.

Apr 1, 1979

Joy Division begin recording "Unkown Pleasures"

Oct 1, 1979

Joy Division tour as the supporting band with The Buzzcocks on a 24 venue UK tour. In November 1979 their single "Transmission" is released.

Jan 1, 1980

European Tour and begin recording for "Closer".

Ian Curtis' health and depression worsen, in part due to lack of sleep and long hours, strong medication for his epilepsy and marital troubles.

May 18, 1980

Ian Curtis died by suicide on the eve of Joy Division's first North American tour, shortly before the release of "Closer".

Jun 1, 1980

Single "Love Will Tear Us Apart" released. The single hits number 13 on the British charts.

Jul 1, 1980

"Closer" is released to critical acclaim.

Jan 1, 1981

Remaining members of Joy Division become New Order. Their first single is "Ceremony". Two years later, New Order release "Blue Monday" which goes on to become the best selling 12" single of all time. New Order go on to have a long and successful career as dance music pioneers.
